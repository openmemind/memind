Payment API Incident Retrospective

Date: 2026-03-28

Summary: a partner retry storm overwhelmed payment-api after clients ignored the Retry-After header. The incident produced a sustained mix of 429 and 503 responses and amplified write pressure on the billing database.

What Failed

  • Several mobile clients retried without exponential backoff.
  • Retry-After compliance dashboards were not included in the release watch list.
  • On-call escalation started too late because the first page waited for absolute error volume.

What To Watch Next Time

  • payment_api.http_429_rate and payment_api.http_503_rate
  • gateway.retry_after_applied and client retry burst dashboards
  • billing_db.replica_lag_seconds and billing write latency p95

Operational Rule

If 429 responses stay above 2% for 10 minutes, or if Retry-After compliance drops sharply, page the Payments API on-call, open the incident bridge, and have the release commander decide whether to pause or rollback the rollout.
